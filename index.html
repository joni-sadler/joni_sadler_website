Joni Sadler

1986 - 2021

Joni Sadler passed away in her sleep—suddenly and without warning—of a brain aneurysm on May 30, 2021

It pains us beyond words to announce this.


It’s impossible to know how Joni would, or would not, have wanted to be remembered in death, because she was too busy living. What she would have wanted is to be alive. To grab a drink and see a show. To hug her friends. To tour Europe with her band Lungbutter, as she was set to do just before the pandemic. Without her, we can only believe she would want us to be together, to live life with purpose, and to be there for each other, like she was for us time and time and time again.

There’s no way to encapsulate the extent of Joni’s impact on our lives. She was grounded, focused, and intensely driven. Her quiet, thoughtful exterior belied the sheer force of will she harnessed to rally people with disparate interests around common goals – and to lift up the voices of independent musicians, in particular. Her fierce friendship and superhuman drive simply do not fit into the tiny box of words required in situations like this. For 35 years, she lived life to the fullest, and then some. She was not done yet.

The only child of entrepreneurs who loved road-tripping, Joni took up figure skating at a young age; she was a competitor bent on excellence and self-improvement. That drive soon found even more purpose in the music and DIY punk scene in her hometown of Victoria, British Columbia. At 18, she moved across the country to attend Carleton University in Ottawa, where she worked at indie record store Sounds Unlikely and as the English Music Director of CHUO 89.1 FM, the campus-community radio station at the University of Ottawa. She completed her BA in 2008 and MA in 2011, both in Communications; her Master’s thesis “Challenging Expectations: Perceptions Of Canadian Music and the Localized Practices of Experimental Artists” should be required reading for anyone interested in the importance of authentic and defiant do-it-yourself music scenes – an astute, accomplished work that has only gained relevance and salience in the decade since she wrote it. Joni then moved to Montreal to co-manage CKUT 90.3 FM at McGill University (from 2012 to 2017). She did a stint as POP Montreal’s Symposium Director (2017 and 2018) and became Director of Communications (though really, a position with more definitions, strands and responsibilities than can be encapsulated in any job title) at venerable independent record label Constellation (2017 to 2021).

COVID-19 has isolated us all. Joni approached the pandemic with immense caution, and would never begrudge anyone for needing to take care of themselves in the ways that make sense to them. She did the same. She missed her parents, friends, bandmates, and colleagues, but she also got immense satisfaction from working hard and pushing herself to do better. She didn’t do it for recognition, she did it on principle. During the pandemic, she threw herself into work on Constellation’s Corona Borealis series of releases to directly benefit artists and expanded the label’s indie platforms, while also learning to code JavaScript, React.js, Node.js, MongoDB, Express, HTML, CSS. Joni had just earned Concordia University’s Full Stack Web Development Diploma, and she was looking forward to the challenges these new skills would bring. Already, she was putting them to use in the redesign of the Constellation website and replacement of their web/email servers. Joni grabbed absolutely everything thrown her way – and dumped in her lap – and wrangled it.

Richard, her partner, is working with Joni's parents, Doug and Kathy, and her friends on memorial arrangements. Given the circumstances, and the ongoing pandemic, please respect the time required to take care of everything, safely and respectfully. Eventually, there will be larger public gatherings and memorial events, organised and announced when conditions permit.
